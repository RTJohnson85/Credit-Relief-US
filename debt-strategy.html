Debt Strategy

The right debt strategy is crucial to success. At U.S. Credit Relief, we work with consumers just like you to build a custom debt strategy based on your unique situation, types of debt, income level, goals, and other factors. To accelerate your debt relief, we will use a unique combination of industry-best debt-relief tools.

Our Debt Relief Solutions and Tools

Our certified debt relief specialists can use a wide range of tools to help you

Type Details Benefits Drawbacks
Debt Validation Debt validation is a legal process that forces your creditors to prove that the debt in question does belong to you and that they have the right to collect it. Under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act, debt collectors are required to adhere to strict requirements. We identify missing account details and other discrepancies and then require your creditors to submit to the debt validation process.
  • Dismiss debts that cannot be validated
  • Reduce your monthly payments
  • Regain financial stability and stop living paycheck to paycheck
  • Not all consumers are eligible
  • A complicated process that makes it less than friendly for DIY purposes
Credit Counseling One of our experienced credit counselors will work with you, review your financial situation, and negotiate lower interest rates with your creditors (where possible). Next, we set up a debt management plan that helps you reduce what you owe.
  • A single monthly payment
  • Reduced interest rates and fees
  • Say goodbye to harassing creditor calls and letters
  • Does not reduce the principal debt amount
  • Credit card accounts will be closed
  • This may increase your risk in the eyes of some lenders
Debt Settlement First, we work with your creditors to negotiate an amount that’s less than what you owe. We also create an account for you that you’ll deposit money into each month. Once we reach an agreement with your creditors, they are paid from your account until the debt is eliminated.
  • Pay off your debt faster than by making minimum payments
  • The chance to pay less than what you owe
  • One monthly payment
  • You’ll still receive debt collection calls
  • Debt settlement affects your credit score
  • Your creditors may not agree to a lower amount
Debt Consolidation Debt consolidation involves taking out a loan large enough to pay off all your debts. The loan will have a low interest rate and you will make fixed monthly payments until you pay the loan off.
  • You get a single, predictable monthly payment every month
  • Pay off your creditors now
  • Does not impact your credit
  • You must have good credit to qualify for the consolidation loan
  • Your principal debt is not reduced
  • The results can vary
Cash-Out Refinance Why not put the equity in your home to work on your behalf? A cash-out refinance allows you to tap into your home’s equity and then use that cash to pay off your debts. You’ll then repay the refinance loan through regular monthly payments.
  • Pay off all your debts at one time
  • Put your home’s equity to use
  • Does not affect your credit
  • Predictable monthly payment
  • You must have sufficient home equity
  • New terms on your home mortgage
Bankruptcy Bankruptcy is a powerful tool in your quest to eliminate debt. Depending on your financial situation and current assets, you might benefit from Chapter 7 or Chapter 13. Filing bankruptcy allows you to have some debts dismissed entirely.
  • You will be able to clear up your debt problems
  • Creditors cannot communicate with you or try to collect on the debt
  • The process is usually fast, often requiring only a few months
  • Stays on your credit report for many years
  • Some consumers may not qualify for Chapter 7
  • You will lose your credit cards
Do it yourself You can create your own debt strategy and try to negotiate lower interest rates or reduced principal on your own. You’ll also find online tools that allow you to track your progress and even determine the specific payments required for each creditor.
  • No additional costs
  • You may not see a reduction in interest or principal
  • You work with your creditors to determine the optimum payment
  • You must negotiate on your own
  • You may not see a reduction in interest or principal
  • You’ll need to stick to a very strict budget
  • You will still have multiple payments per month

Bankruptcy

Bankruptcy is a legal process that allows you to settle your debts, and in some cases, have them dismissed entirely. Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 are the two most commonly used types of bankruptcy, but they are drastically different. Bankruptcy will also stay on your credit report for 7 – 10 years and will affect your financial future and even your employment prospects.
