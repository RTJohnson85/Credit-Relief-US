Refinancing Your Debts

Too many people assume that once they’ve signed the paperwork for a loan, they’re locked in. The truth is that’s rarely the case, at least when it comes to loans rather than credit cards. Loans can often be refinanced, which helps you achieve many different benefits, including reducing your interest rate and obtaining better terms overall.

Not sure if refinancing is right for your needs? Contact us ! One of our expert team members will be happy to discuss your options and create a customized plan tailored to your unique needs and financial goals.

Why Should I Consider Refinancing My Debt?

Why refinance your debt? The average American shoulders almost $100,000 in consumer debt, and refinancing can help make managing that burden easier. Some of the more common reasons for refinancing a loan include:

  • Reducing interest rates
  • Reducing monthly payment amounts
  • To consolidate other debts
  • To reduce your risk by switching to a different rate type (from variable to fixed-rate, for instance)
  • To access equity built up in a home and then use that cash to consolidate higher-interest rate debt

Is Refinancing Right for Me?

Refinancing can be a smart decision, particularly if you are in a position to obtain a lower interest rate or you need to access the equity that you’ve built up over time. However, it’s not right for everyone. One of our debt experts will work with you to determine if this is the right path.

It’s also important to realize that many lenders require a percentage of the total loan amount paid upfront. Today, lenders use “points” instead of dollar amounts to specify how much is required. A single point is usually equal to 1% of the total loan amount, so if the lender requires 2 points to refinance, you know that you’ll need to provide 2% or more of the total loan amount at the time of refinancing.
