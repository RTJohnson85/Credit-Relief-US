Debt Settlement

Professional Debt Settlement for Your Financial Challenges

According to the Federal Reserve, Americans have racked up an incredible $14.6 trillion in debt. The average American shoulders almost $100,000 in debt between credit cards, mortgages, auto loans, student loans, and the rest. It’s an immense burden, and it can feel like there’s no way out. Thankfully, debt settlement can give you the fresh start that you need.

What Is Debt Settlement?

If you’re struggling with mounting debt and can’t seem to find the way out, debt settlement could be exactly what you need. This is a debt relief solution that allows you to pay less than what you owe to your creditors through what’s called a debt reduction plan. .

The challenge is that you must negotiate with each creditor to first get them onboard with the plan and then to arrive at a figure that benefits you rather than them. At U.S. Credit Relief, our skilled negotiators go to the table with your creditors to reach an agreement that helps you slash your debt burden by as much as 50% before fees.

How Does the Process Work?

The first step is to decide that this is the path for you. You’re able to negotiate your debt on your own, but it requires time and the ability to communicate effectively with aggressive debt collection representatives and creditors. We specialize in representing people just like you in these negotiations.

To negotiate your debt settlement agreement, you will need to meet with each creditor independently. During each meeting, you would need to negotiate a lower amount and then have each creditor sign an agreement that spells out the terms.

Working with a specialist can streamline the process, allow you to focus on more important things in your life, and give you an edge in the negotiation process. Our process looks like this:

  • Claim your free consultation, during which we’ll learn about you, your debt burden, and your frustrations with creditors.
  • We’ll create a customized debt reduction plan that meets your specific needs and goals.
  • You’ll review the plan and approve it.
  • You’ll begin paying funds into an FDIC-insured escrow account.
  • Those funds will eventually go to pay your creditors once we have negotiated a debt settlement agreement.

Will Your Creditors Accept Less Than What You Owe?

Creditors often take a hard line when dealing with consumers. However, working with a specialist like U.S. Credit Relief gives you an edge. During our negotiations, we inform your creditors that there are limited funds for repayment, and we’ve found that most creditors are willing to accept some money, rather than no money.

Is Debt Settlement Right for You?

Debt settlement can help you regain control over your financial life. If you have $10,000 or more in unsecured debt and want to reduce what you owe by up to 50%, this could be the path for you. Contact us today to schedule your consultation.
